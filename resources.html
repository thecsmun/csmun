Liability & Consent Form

All registered delegates are required to download, complete, and submit a signed hard copy of the Liability and Consent Declaration Form at the registration desk upon arrival at the conference venue. This form must be duly signed by the delegate and, for participants under 18 years of age, co-signed by a parent or legal guardian.

Please Note: Delegates will not be permitted to participate in conference sessions without submitting the completed form.

Conference Policies

📜 Code of Conduct

All delegates are expected to uphold the highest standards of professionalism and integrity throughout the conference.

  1. Respect all participants and maintain professionalism at all times.
  2. Use diplomatic language and adhere to parliamentary procedure.
  3. Adhere strictly to the formal dress code throughout all sessions.
  4. Attend all committee sessions punctually and remain engaged.
  5. Use electronic devices responsibly and only when permitted by the Chair.
  6. Avoid plagiarism and maintain academic integrity in all written submissions.
  7. Refrain from harassment, discrimination, or any form of disruptive behavior.
  8. Follow the directions of the Executive Board and Secretariat at all times.
  9. Maintain the decorum and reputation of CSMUN and your institution.
  10. Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action, including removal from the conference and disqualification from awards.

👔 Dress Code Policy

Formal attire is mandatory for all delegates throughout the duration of CSMUN 2026.

  • Male Delegates: Formal business suit, dress shirt, necktie, and formal leather shoes (black or brown).
  • Female Delegates: Business suit, formal dress, or traditional formal attire with appropriate closed-toe formal footwear.
  • Lok Sabha Delegates: Indian traditional formal attire (such as kurta-pyjama or saree) is permitted and encouraged.
  • Prohibited: Casual wear, jeans, t-shirts, sneakers, sandals, or any inappropriate attire.
  • Consequences: Delegates not adhering to the dress code may be denied entry to committee sessions.

📱 Electronic Device Policy

  • The use of laptops and tablets is permitted only during unmoderated caucuses and for the purpose of drafting resolutions and working papers.
  • The use of mobile phones during formal committee sessions is strictly prohibited.
  • Delegates may not use electronic devices for personal communication, social media, or entertainment during sessions.
  • The Executive Board reserves the right to confiscate devices being misused.
